What We Watch For

Signs a Child May Need an Orthodontic Assessment

Crowded or Erupting Teeth

When space is limited, teeth may erupt irregularly or overlap early.

Bite and Jaw Imbalance

Crossbite, overbite, and jaw discrepancies are easier to evaluate during growth phases.

Habits and Functional Patterns

Thumb sucking, mouth breathing, or other habits can affect growth and alignment.

Treatment Stages

Early Guidance, Teen Braces, and Long-Term Monitoring

Observation Phase

Sometimes the right answer is simply to review growth and wait for better timing.

Interceptive Treatment

Some cases benefit from early intervention to reduce future crowding or bite severity.

Teen Orthodontics

Once all factors are ready, children may transition into braces or aligner planning.

FAQs

Common Questions About Pediatric Orthodontics in Delhi

At what age should a child first see an orthodontist?

An early assessment is often useful when adult teeth start erupting and bite issues become visible, even if treatment is not started immediately.

Does every child need braces?

No. Some children need monitoring only, while others benefit from early intervention or later full treatment.

Can teens get Invisalign?

Some teenagers are suitable candidates, depending on compliance and treatment goals.
